JASON REDMOND

Welcome to my web page! My name is Jason Redmond, and I am an attorney transitioning into the world of programming. While I have loved using computers since infancy, I didn't seriously consider a career in this field until recently. I completed a rigorous Full-Stack Development Bootcamp at Skill Distillery in 2020, and am now trained in the following skills:

  • Java/JavaEE
  • JavaScript / TypeScript
  • HTML5
  • CSS3
  • JDBC
  • Spring Boot
  • JPQL
  • RESTful API
  • JSON
  • Git / GitHub
  • ...and learning more every day!

Please take a look around, and get to know me a little better. This is an ongoing project, so visit frequently!

I am originally from Michigan, but spent the majority of my adult life in the state of Georgia. There, I (briefly) attended Georgia Tech, received my undergraduate degree at UGA, and obtained my law degree at Mercer University. I promptly passed the Georgia Bar, and began my adventure into adulthood.

In 2016 I finally acted on a long-standing craving for a change (and colder weather), and relocated to Colorado. Since then, I was admitted to the Colorado Bar, and briefly continued my pursuits in law. However, my desire for a change slowly crept into my career as well, and I ultimately walked away...

I realized I didn't like the destructive and divisive nature of my profession, and wanted a career that allowed for more collaboration and creation. Having spoken with many of my friends in the IT industries, it became apparent to me that coding and software engineering would present me with better opportunities to utilize my legal skills for a more constructive purpose. Furthermore, I had been inseparable from computers since I was 5, so it only made sense to pursue this route of study.

In 2020, I officially took the plunge and began my transition into computer programming. I began dabbling in some online tutorials and classes while I researched the various coding bootcamps available to me. I ultimately decided on Skill Distillery, based on the recommendations of a few friends who worked with graduates from the program. I was also given positive feedback from other friends in the industry who felt that the curriculum offered a legitimate foundation for full-stack development.

After a few logisitical hiccups from the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic, I started my program in August 2020. From Day 1 I was given rigorous training with daily 10-hour classes requiring constant absorption of new materials and concepts. I readily devoured the information, always looking forward to the weekend projects that would allow me to test what I had learned.

16 weeks of hard work later, I graduated with a few full-stack projects under my belt. I. Loved. Every. Minute. Of. It.

Armed with this training and experience, I am now thrust again into the real world. I look forward to the challenges ahead, and the endless opportunities to increase my knowledge.
